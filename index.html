About the
Varsity Show

The Varsity Show is an annual, full-length musical comedy satirizing life at Columbia, conceived and performed by students to unite the campus community in laughter and reflection on the past year's events.

Founded in 1894, the show is Columbia's most beloved performing arts tradition and is widely regarded as one of the highlights of the academic year.

The Varsity Show has, for generations, served as an early springboard for the careers of some of the world's finest and most celebrated artists, including Richard Rodgers, Oscar Hammerstein II, Tom Kitt, Greta Gerwig, Jenny Slate, Kate McKinnon, among many others.
